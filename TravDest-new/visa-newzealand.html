New Zealand Visa Requirements

    A guide for applicants

    It is important that you provide all the relevant information requested below so we can accept and process your application promptly. If any of the following are not received, the application may not be accepted for assessment.


    All documents not in English must be translated by a translation service. A Translation Service may NOT certify documents as original.


    Application forms can be downloaded and other relevant information may be found on our website www.immigration.govt.nz.


    Application Form


    An Application for Visiting New Zealand must be fully completed in English. Answer each question on the form and continue that question on a separate sheet if necessary. A couple and their dependent children 19 years of age and under may be included on the same application form.


    APPLICATION FEE

    The application fee is a processing fee and is NON-REFUNDABLE regardless of the outcome of your visa application. You have a choice of paying either in Thai Baht or in US Dollars. The details for each are given below:


    The current application fee is THB 3,300 Thai Baht. The fee can be paid by bank draft or by cashier cheque when you apply in person. Bank Drafts should be made payable to New Zealand Immigration Service.
    OR

    The current application fee in US dollars is US$115.00 Approx. (including bank clearance fees). Please note the fee must be paid by bank draft in US dollars only, drawn from a US bank, made payable to the New Zealand Immigration Service.


    We also accept Visa and MasterCard. Cash is NOT accepted by this office.



    Photo

    One recent passport sized photo (must be less than 6 months old) of each person included in the application.



    Passport or travel document

    Passports must be valid for 3 months beyond intended date of departure from New Zealand. We will accept the application with a certified copy of the bio-data page of the passport but we will require the actual passport for 24 hours before a visa is issued.


    Evidence of support

    Evidence of funds to support yourself which must be a minimum of NZ$1,000.00 per month of stay. This can be in the form of bank records, cash, travellers cheques or credit card statements.


    OR

    A Sponsorship Form (NZIS1025) completed by a New Zealand citizen or resident along with evidence of their ability to support you while you are in New Zealand.



    Additional supporting Information

    If travelling on business or attending a conference, evidence of business contacts in New Zealand such as invitations or correspondence with New Zealand business parties are required. This information is not required if you have travelled previously to New Zealand on business.


    To enable us to make an informed decision, please provide any additional information which proves you will be a genuine short-term visitor to New Zealand, and shows you have incentive to return to your home country after visiting New Zealand such as


    A letter of leave from your employer or school. Letters from an employer should include annual income (including bonus), length of employment and position held. Also, it must be on company letterhead, bearing the stamp of the company and the signature of the head of the company or the responsible person of the personnel department. If you are self-employed, business or company registration certificate showing proof of ownership and company bank statements must be submitted.


    Evidence of any previous travel i.e. clear copy of visas on passport.


    Your travel/flight bookings/itinerary/tickets if available
    Children under the age of 16 years travelling alone or with only 1 parent must provide a written letter of consent from both parents which include the parents contact details and identification.


    X-ray for Tuberculosis screening

    Applicants for temporary entry to New Zealand must have an acceptable standard of health


    All applicants from a high incidence TB country, intending to be in New Zealand for more than 6 months must undergo a TB screening and provide a Chest X-ray certificate (NZIS1096) completed by an approved INZ Panel Doctor. Please check our website for a list of high incidence TB countries and to find the Panel Doctor nearest to you.


    People who have spent a combined total of 3 months or more (in the five years before the application) in any high incidence TB country will also need to be tested for TB.


    Pregnant women and children below the age of 11 years are not required to have a chest x-ray.



    Other Requirements

    FULL MEDICAL Applicants intending to spend more than 12 months in New Zealand are required to supply a full medical certificate (NZIS1007). Pregnant women and children below the age of 11 years are not required to have a chest x-ray.


    The list of appointed panel doctor :

    Bangkok Global Doctor, Bangkok Nursing Home Hospital, Bamrungrad Hospital, Samitivej Hospital


    Chiang Mai Wattana-Niramon Clinic, Special Medical Service Centre (Faculty of Medicine, Chiangmai University), Drs. Vanachantra-Charas Clinic


    Khon Kaen Srinagarind Hospital, Khon Kaen Ram Hospital



    Police Clearance

    Applicants over 17 years intending to remain in New Zealand for more than 24 months are required to supply a police clearance certificate from their country of residence and any country the applicant has been in for five years or more since turning 17.
